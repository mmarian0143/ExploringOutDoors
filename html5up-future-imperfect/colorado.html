MOUNT SNEFFELS, COLORADO

Lower Blue Lake

August 28, 2020 Madalina Marian

Colorado is one of the most beautiful states I've been to so far. Every corner you turn, there's a scenery to see. I also locate in Arizona, so being able to wake up to a fifty-degree weather in the middle of a summer was a bonus for me. This hike was located in a glacial basin in southwest Colorado. There are three different lakes there, but I chose to hike to the lowest one. And I'll be honest, it was a cold and difficult 6.9-mile hike, but it was so worth it. Not only was the destination breathtaking, but so were the scenery before even reaching the lake. One of the worst decisions I made when hiking this was not bringing along a snack, so don't make that same mistake. Even if you have just a few almonds or peanuts...bring them along so you won't get hangry on the way. Most of the hike was steep so I wouldn't recommend bringing children along. In the future, I would like to hike the whole mountain which is about 13 miles with an elevation gain of 5,501 ft... but I would also bring along my camping gear and camp out at each lake before continuing to the next. That way I have time to enjoy the views while also hiking one of the tallest peaks in the state of Colorado.

The best thing to do when you visit this state is to rent a car and travel all around the state that way you don't miss out on any breathtaking looks. Because taking a plane to travel around it would only allow you to see not even half of it's beauty.
